Twelve findings, each written for the person who can act on it

Numbers computed from the full six-and-a-half-year fact table, weekdays benchmarked against February 2020. An insight isn't "what the data says" — it's what you should do differently after reading it.

Property investors
72% / 136%

North Sydney is two markets now

Weekday demand is down a quarter; Saturdays run at 136% of 2020. Office values priced on commuter footfall deserve a discount — but ground-floor tenancy re-mixed toward weekend hospitality is the repositioning play. Screen conversion candidates here first.

Retail leasing
+49%

Lease for Saturday night, not Monday lunch

CBD Saturday evenings beat pre-COVID by half. If your tenancy mix still targets the office worker (dry cleaner, salad bar, 9–5 trading), you're leasing to a shrinking customer. Late-trading F&B justifies premium rents now.

Office landlords
4.8 days

Underwrite a 4.8-day city

Monday runs 15% below midweek and isn't coming back. Don't model occupancy recovery to 2019 in your DCF; do model flight-to-quality — tenants consolidating four good days in better buildings.

Transport planners
−25%

The 5pm crunch case for reallocating service

The evening peak shrank twice as fast as total demand. Capacity built for the 2019 peak is idle capital: the service case has shifted to evenings, weekends and the shoulder hours where demand now grows.

Precinct policy
45→66%

Fund the visitor city — it's working

CBD weekends went from 45% to 66% of weekday volume. Activation spending has a measurable return; the binding constraint is now late-night transport home, not reasons to come in.

Site selection
2×

Pay the light-rail corridor premium

Light rail doubled its network share (7.6→14.1%) while bus and train drifted down. For QSR and convenience formats, corridor stops are where incremental foot traffic is actually appearing.

Contrarian check
16.8→14.8%

Don't overpay for the "western CBD" story yet

Parramatta's volume as a share of the CBD's fell since 2020. The growth narrative may be right long-term, but transit demand says it isn't here yet — negotiate entry prices accordingly.

Risk & scenario teams
6%

Your stress test has a floor: it's 6%

The worst weekday hit 6% of normal; the worst month (Aug 2021, Delta) was 11% — deeper than 2020. Use these as the realistic tail scenario for any footfall-dependent revenue model, and note the second shock cut deeper than the first.

F&B operators
16→19.5%

Staff for a plateau, not a spike

Midday grew from 16% to 19.5% of CBD weekday taps: the day is flatter. Roster and inventory built around two sharp peaks now mismatch how demand actually arrives — spread it, especially Tuesday to Thursday.

Tourism & harbourside assets
+44%

The harbour is a weekend business now

Weekend ferry taps beat pre-COVID by 44% and now out-traffic weekdays. For Circular Quay and Manly retail, weight your covenant analysis toward leisure spend, not commuter convenience.

Valuers & CFOs
+3.5 pts/yr

No rebound left to underwrite

83% → 86% → 90% over three years: a crawl, not a wave. Any valuation or budget that assumes a snap back to 2019 is booking revenue that arrives, at best, around 2029. Hybrid is the baseline case.

4 · The 167% that wasn't

Wollongong's "recovery" was the most exciting number in this dataset — which is exactly why it deserved interrogation first.

Bus taps tripled. Trains didn't move.

Annual tap-ons (millions), Wollongong & surrounds. 2026 is a half year. Organic demand does not grow like the orange line.

Method. Three checks: (1) imputation sensitivity — setting every privacy-masked cell to 0 or to its ceiling moves the figure only 166–168%, so masking isn't the cause; (2) mode decomposition — bus 1.16M→3.78M in three years while train held ~1M, the signature of a reporting-coverage change; (3) no public announcement found to explain it as demand. Verdict: flagged as a structural break and excluded from headline comparisons. Newcastle is excluded too — at 52–58% masked, its recovery reads anywhere from 77% to 109% depending on assumptions.

Method notes. Source: TfNSW Opal Patronage open dataset (CC-BY), hourly tap-ons/offs by mode and commercial centre, 2020-01-01 → 2026-07-01. Values under the privacy threshold (<50 to Jun 2024, <100 after — a mid-series change) are midpoint-imputed and flagged; aggregate rows and the UNKNOWN mode are excluded. Pipeline: Python (pandas/pyarrow) bulk ingest → schema validation → fact table → this page (ECharts + Leaflet, no framework). Full QA notes and code on GitHub.
